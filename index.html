    Our Popular Places

    Lalibela, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is famous for its stunning rock-hewn churches carved in the 12th century, reflecting Ethiopia's rich religious heritage. Nearby, Axum showcases ancient obelisks and the Church of St. Mary of Zion, believed to house the Ark of the Covenant. The breathtaking landscapes of Simien Mountains National Park and the historic castles of Gondar further highlight Ethiopia's remarkable cultural and natural beauty.

    • Lalibela

      Famed for its rock-hewn churches, Lalibela is often referred to as the "New Jerusalem." This UNESCO World Heritage site features 11 monolithic churches carved from solid rock, showcasing incredible engineering and artistry. Pilgrims and tourists alike are drawn to this spiritual center, especially during Ethiopian Orthodox festivals.

    • Simien Mountains National Park

      A UNESCO World Heritage site, Simien Mountains National Park boasts dramatic landscapes, including rugged peaks and deep valleys. Hikers can explore its stunning trails, while wildlife enthusiasts may spot endemic species like the Gelada baboon and the Ethiopian wolf. The breathtaking views make it a must-visit for nature lovers.

    • Gondar

      Known for its medieval castles, Gondar is a city rich in history. The Royal Enclosure, a UNESCO World Heritage site, features impressive structures like the Fasil Ghebbi and the Church of Debre Birhan Selassie, adorned with beautiful murals. Gondar's vibrant culture and festivals make it a captivating destination.

    • Bahir Dar and Lake Tana

      Bahir Dar is a picturesque city located on the shores of Lake Tana, Ethiopia's largest lake. Visitors can take boat trips to explore the many islands and ancient monasteries, such as the ones on Dek Island. The nearby Blue Nile Falls, known as Tis Issat, is a stunning natural attraction worth visiting.
